GRC | Rain Gear E-commerce Website

Graha Retalindo Cemerlang is a distributor company specialising in rain gears such as raincoats, umbrellas, rain boots, etc. Targeting a large market accommodating kids to adults.

Details

Target Market:
20 - 30 year old

The Challenge:
Creating a beautiful and professional looking e-commerce website to showcase the wide range of products GRC offers.

Outcome:
A responsive website with a bold and clean design.

Length:
4-6 weeks

Role:
Graphic & UI Designer (research, visual design, design system) at VOIA Studio, working alongside a Creative Director, and a programmer to make our vision a reality!

It Started as a Branding Project

Initially started out as a branding project, I was tasked as the Graphic Designer to redesign GRC’s old logo so it looks more fresh and up to date with the younger market. After successfully designing the logo and brand attributes, I continued on to making a catalog book for GRC, the photoshoots are done by my colleagues at VOIA Studio, and I created the layout design for the catalog book.

Getting into UI-
Research

As we finished with the catalog design, we proceeded into making the e-commerce website. As you might already recognise as a pattern by now, my working process begins with research as follows:

  1. Defining the Client’s needs, business goals, and Identity.
  2. Understanding what factors came into play to create a professional, clean and modern looking e-commerce website.
  3. Looking at market competitors and design trends to determine design style and direction.
  4. Understanding user behaviour and creating the best shopping experience.

Initial research:
Customer Insight

the first phase of research was to find information and understanding about the Client, their goals, and their needs.

Secondary Research:
Market Competitors

The second phase of research was to understand and determine the standards of e-commerce apps, as well as retail online shops. The typical look and feel, and figure out how to improve and deliver better results. thenorthface.com
patagonia.com
kuiu.com
hellyhansen.com
carhartt.com

User Flow & Wireframes

Next, with the information gathered from the first two researches, we proceeded to create the User Flow & Wireframes.

Visual Design

After determining and creating the User flow and wireframes, we can move on to developing the key visuals and exploring the design on Sketch.

Design System

After designing several pages digitally and getting fine tune adjustments from both the Client and Creative Director, I proceed to create the UI kit, consisting of components such as buttons, states, color palette, text styles, and pixel perfect icons. These are all then passed on to the Programmer, and he develops the website.

Final Design

and finally, the final look of GRC’s website!
